This project implements a Swin UNet model for automatic segmentation of the knee joint from MRI scans, followed by a 3D reconstruction of anatomical structures. Swin UNet leverages the power of transformer-based architectures for improved contextual understanding in medical image segmentation.

Swin UNet is based on the Swin Transformer backbone, which applies shifted window attention to efficiently model long-range dependencies in the image. It maintains a hierarchical representation and integrates it into a UNet-like encoder-decoder design.
